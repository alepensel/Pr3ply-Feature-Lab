# Discovery

## Framing

Pr3ply already validates that learners will book 1-on-1 sessions at a premium. The open question is whether a **tutor-moderated group format** can produce comparable perceived value at a lower per-seat price, and thereby unlock more tutor-hour revenue.

## Target users

- **Learners:** working adults, B1–C1 English, using English for job, travel, or interviews. Price-sensitive relative to 1-on-1. Willing to speak in front of a small group if the environment feels safe.
- **Tutors:** professional teachers already earning on 1-on-1; want to lift utilization on off-peak hours without cannibalizing 1-on-1 revenue.

## Jobs to be done

- Learner: *"When I have 45 minutes free after work, help me practice speaking English in a realistic scenario and tell me what to fix, so I feel measurable progress this week."*
- Learner (secondary): *"When I book, reassure me who else will be in the room, so I don't feel exposed."*
- Tutor: *"When my calendar has a soft hour, help me fill it with a group session that runs itself — I moderate, structure comes from the tool, feedback writes itself."*

## Key pain points

- Existing group formats degrade into free-form video calls; learners hesitate to speak; strong speakers dominate.
- No structured artifact after a session, so it's hard to feel progress.
- Tutors don't have time to write per-student feedback for 2–4 learners per session.
- Learners hesitate to book if the "who's in the room" question is opaque.

## Hypotheses

- **H1:** learners will rebook a group session within 7 days at ≥ 25% rate if they receive a specific, actionable AI feedback report.
- **H2:** tutor-controlled turn-taking (speaker selection + visible timer) meaningfully reduces speaker-dominance imbalance vs. a plain Meet call.
- **H3:** AI-generated role-play prompts, when conditioned on level + scenario, are rated ≥ 4/5 for relevance by learners.
- **H4:** publishing the participant list on the session detail page increases booking conversion vs. hiding it.

## Validation plan

- **Prototype attendance test:** run 10–15 curated sessions with the current MVP; measure booked-to-attended rate.
- **Feedback usefulness survey:** 1-question micro-survey on the feedback page after MVP launch.
- **Rebook cohort tracking:** measure 7-day and 30-day rebook rate for MVP cohorts.
- **Comparative session recordings (opt-in):** manual review of 5 sessions with the rail vs. 5 without to sanity-check H2.

## Sample interview questions

For learners:

1. Walk me through the last time you practiced speaking English. What was the setup, what worked, what didn't?
2. How do you decide whether a session was "worth it"?
3. What would need to be true for you to book a group session over a 1-on-1?
4. If you got a feedback report after a session, what would you want it to say? What would make you delete it?
5. Under what conditions would you not want to be in a room with strangers?

For tutors:

1. What's the hardest part of running a group class?
2. If a tool could take one thing off your plate during or after a session, what would give you the most time back?
3. How would you decide whether to run more group hours instead of 1-on-1?

## What this prototype is designed to test

- The **in-room learning layer** (rail + tutor turn-taking + timer) as a differentiator vs. a plain video call.
- The **post-session feedback + rebook loop** as a retention driver.
- **Publishing the participant list** as a booking-conversion lever.

Not tested by this prototype: pricing elasticity, marketplace supply dynamics, tutor payout satisfaction, peer-only (tutor-less) format.
